## Primary AI Generation Prompt

Create a conceptual diagram showing Bitcoin's unique balance between privacy (pseudonymity) and transparency (public ledger), displayed as a balanced scale or yin-yang style visualization on a pure black background (#000000). The composition should show two complementary aspects: Left/Top half: "Public Transparency" - Represent the blockchain as an open ledger visible to all. Show this as a transparent/glass ledger or open book icon in muted blue (#4477FF) with transaction records visible as lines of data. Include symbolic indicators: "All transactions public", "Network verification possible", "Auditable supply". Right/Bottom half: "Private Identity" - Represent pseudonymity as masked addresses. Show this as wallet addresses (strings like "1A1zP1...") in gold (#FFD000) with no connection to real-world identities. Include symbolic indicators: "No names attached", "Addresses pseudonymous", "Identity optional". In the center where these two halves meet, show a balance point or equilibrium symbol, emphasizing that Bitcoin achieves both: public verification + private identity. Add a title: "Bitcoin's Transparency Paradox: Public Ledger, Private Identity". Below the diagram, include a key insight: "Unlike bank accounts (private ledger, known identity) or fully anonymous cash, Bitcoin is verifiable yet pseudonymous." The style should be conceptual infographic with philosophical depth - explaining a nuanced technical/social property. No photographs, no faces - focused on the architectural balance between competing values. The visual should help users understand this often-confusing aspect of Bitcoin.

## Alternative Prompt Variations

### Variation A (Three-Way Comparison):
Show three systems side-by-side: (1) Traditional Banking: Private ledger (hidden) + Known identity (exposed), (2) Cash: Private transaction (hidden) + Anonymous (unknown), (3) Bitcoin: Public ledger (visible) + Pseudonymous (masked). Shows Bitcoin's unique position.

### Variation B (Onion Layers):
Show blockchain transparency as outer layer (visible to all, blue) with pseudonymous addresses as inner layer (gold), and optional identity as innermost layer (user choice, white). Layered privacy model. Shows separation of transaction data and identity.

### Variation C (Lock and Glass):
Left: Open glass safe showing contents (public ledger, transparent). Right: Mask or unlabeled key (private identity). Shows coexistence of transparency and privacy. Simple metaphorical visualization.

## Educational Note

This is a nuanced topic that many beginners misunderstand. The visualization should:
1. **Correct misconception:** Bitcoin is not "anonymous" like cash or Monero
2. **Explain transparency:** All transactions are public and verifiable
3. **Show privacy:** Addresses don't inherently reveal identity
4. **Emphasize choice:** Users can maintain privacy or disclose identity

Avoid any framing that implies Bitcoin is primarily for illicit activity.
